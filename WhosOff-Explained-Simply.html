Our own WhosOff, explained simply

We're building our own staff time-off tracker so we stop paying rent for one and fully own our data. Here's the whole thing in plain English.

1 The big idea

Right now we rent a tool called WhosOff to track who's on holiday, sick, or working from home. Our data lives in their house, and we pay every month.

We're building our own version that lives in our house. It does everything WhosOff does — book leave, approve it, show the team calendar, track overtime, send reminders — but the data is ours and we can change or restyle it whenever we like. Everyone gets in with their Google work login (no new password to remember).

2 Three kinds of people use it

Same app, different powers.

🧑‍💻

You (any employee)

Book time off, see how many days you have left, and check who else is away.

✅

Managers (approvers)

Everything above, plus approve or decline their team's requests and see their team's calendar.

🛠️

HR (the system bosses)

Set everything up: add people, fix balances, set holidays, choose who approves whom, run reports.

3 What you can do

The everyday stuff every employee touches.

🔑

Log in with Google

One click with your work account. No extra sign-up.

🏠

Your home screen

Days left, your next 7 days, and a quick "book time off" box.

📝

Book time off

Pick the type and dates. It checks your balance, warns about clashes, and won't let you book more than you have.

📅

Team wall chart

A big calendar of who's off, filter by team or country.

📜

My Leave

Your full history, with buttons to cancel a booking or nudge your approver.

4 What HR can do

  • Manage everyone — people are pulled in automatically from our Google directory.
  • Set the details — each person's country, team, manager and working days.
  • Fix balances in one click — the system does the maths, no spreadsheets.
  • Set public holidays per country — UAE, KSA, Beirut and Remote all differ.
  • Decide who approves whom — and step in as backup whenever needed.
  • Run reports — balances, who's off, who hasn't taken enough leave.

5 The robot does the boring stuff

Things that happen automatically so no one has to chase them.

  • Counts days correctly for each country (different weekends and holidays).
  • Stops double-bookings and blocks booking more days than you have.
  • Sends notes by email and Microsoft Teams direct message.
  • Posts the Friday "who's off next week" digest.
  • Nudges people sitting on unused leave.
  • Files everyone's yearly record automatically at year end.

7 The rules we've locked in

Decisions already made, in plain words.

  • You get your whole year of days on 1 January (new joiners get a fair share for their part of the year).
  • Leftover days can roll over — and HR sets the rule for each market (how many, and when they expire).
  • You can't book more days than you have.
  • Time off is in full or half days (no by-the-hour leave, and no overtime tracking).
  • One approver each; bigger cases can need two yeses; HR can always step in.
  • It runs on Vercel (we already pay for it) and can move to our own server later.
  • Login is Google; uploaded HR documents auto-delete after 2 years.
  • It's all plain software doing the maths — no AI guessing anything.
